Also known as curve stitching or loom art, string art is a captivating geometric craft that uses colored thread or wire strung between fixed points (nails) to create intricate patterns and recognizable images.

Originally developed in the late 19th century to help children understand mathematical concepts like coordinate geometry, it has evolved into a sophisticated form of contemporary art. By layering thousands of individual lines, the human eye begins to perceive depth and texture.

ðŸ”¢ The Mathematics

At its core, string art is the visual representation of a Ruled Surface. By connecting points on two lines with straight threads, the overlapping "envelope" of these lines creates a smooth curve. Our app uses Coordinate Geometry and Greedy Selection to solve the pathing problem.

Algorithm Logic:

"Every straight line contributes to a complex curve; every connection reduces the image's entropy."
